The Assessment Investigator Project

In recent years, a resurgence of a phenomenon known as contract cheating has caused concern amongst academia in higher education, especially at universities. The problem is also referred to as online auction, plagiarism by third parties, plagiarism, and just plain cheating. So, what exactly is contract cheating? Itâ€™s a form of academic dishonesty, where students either purchase a pre-written assignment or have a third-party assist them to a greater or lesser extent in completing an assignment in exchange for some form of payment, rather than completing the work themselves.
To counter contract cheating, Swinburne academics lead by Professor Jun Han, Professor Ron Bartles and Dr. Caslon Chua, had tasked three Swinburne 3rd Year undergraduate students with developing Software which could be used by Swinburne academics to quickly and easily launch an internet search for copies of Swinburne academic papers which had been sold to students.
